# Sports Biomechanics and Physical Therapy Considerations for Shalat

Biomechanics of Ruku (Bowing): Standard Ruku requires near 90-degree forward hip flexion while maintaining a neutral (flat) lumbar spine. Hands should rest firmly on the patellas (kneecaps). If a person has active disc herniation, rounding the lower back (lumbar flexion) during Ruku significantly increases intradiscal pressure and risk of pain.

Anatomical Fix (Lumbar herniation): To reduce pain, focus on "hinging at the hips" by pushing the glutes (hips) straight back, keeping the chest up and the spine flat. Do not round your upper or lower back. If this cue is insufficient, only bend as far as is possible before pain is triggered, or perform the movement seated.

Biomechanics of Sujud (Prostration): Proper Sujud requires distributing body weight across seven points of contact. A beginner who cannot get their palms to the ground effectively may be rounding their back excessively. An improper hand placement (too narrow) can put undue strain on the shoulder joint.
