# General Islamic Jurisprudence Principles on Prayer

Allah intends for you ease and does not intend for you hardship (Quran 2:185).

Fiqh of Ruku (Bowing): The mandatory action (Rukn) is to bend so that the hands reach the knees if physically capable. If a severe chronic injury prevents this, bending only as much as is safely possible is acceptable while maintaining the mental intention of the pillar. The priority is to avoid exacerbating pain.

Fiqh of Sujud (Prostration): According to the Prophetic tradition, prostration must be performed on seven "bones" or parts of the body: the forehead (including the nose), the two palms, the two knees, and the pads of the two feet. If a recent surgical procedure prevents the knees from touching the ground, the worshipper is permitted to sit on a chair and incline their torso forward, making the incline for Sujud deeper than the incline for Ruku.
